# Receipt: Password Spray From Residential Proxies Confirmed

## Claim

An external actor conducted a coordinated password spray attack against 15 meridian.io Okta accounts using rotating residential proxy IPs over an 8-minute window (HYP-01).

## Evidence

**events=1189 (failed auths), templates=3 (total query), entities=15**

Observable facts from QRY-20260329-001:

1. **Volume and timing:** 1,189 failed authentication attempts in 8 minutes 35 seconds (14:00:12Z -- 14:08:47Z). Average rate: 2.3 failures per second.

2. **Distribution pattern:** Failures distributed uniformly across 15 accounts (mean: 79.3, std dev: 4.2). This uniform distribution is the signature of password spray (rotating across accounts) vs. brute force (concentrated on one account).

3. **Source IPs:** 79 unique source IPs, all resolving to residential proxy ASNs:
   - AS396982 (Google Cloud residential proxy tunnel): 31 IPs
   - AS16276 (OVH residential exit node): 24 IPs
   - AS-RESIDENTIAL-PROXY (Luminati/Bright Data network): 24 IPs
   - No IPs overlap with Meridian corporate ranges (203.0.113.0/24) or known VPN endpoints

4. **Credential source pattern:** All 1,189 failures returned `INVALID_CREDENTIALS` (not `PASSWORD_EXPIRED` or `ACCOUNT_LOCKED_OUT` initially), suggesting the attacker had username-password pairs, not just usernames. This is consistent with credential stuffing from a breach database where most passwords have been changed but one (david.park) had not.

5. **Template clustering confirmation:** Drain parser reduced 1,189 raw failure events to a single structural template: `Authentication failed for <EMAIL> from <IP> -- INVALID_CREDENTIALS`. The uniformity of the template confirms automated tooling (no variation in auth flow or error handling).
