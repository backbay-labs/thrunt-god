## Notes

- The absence of the malicious app in Okta logs is a significant finding: it means the attack vector specifically exploited M365's direct OAuth consent flow rather than going through the federated IdP. This has implications for detection engineering — Okta-only monitoring would miss this attack entirely.
- All three users show consistent baseline behavior in Okta: regular business-hours sign-ins from known locations, standard MFA usage, and legitimate OAuth app usage.
- Template 3 (OAuth token grants) in Okta covers only Okta-brokered OAuth flows. M365-native OAuth consent is not visible here, which is why QRY-20260328-001 was necessary.
