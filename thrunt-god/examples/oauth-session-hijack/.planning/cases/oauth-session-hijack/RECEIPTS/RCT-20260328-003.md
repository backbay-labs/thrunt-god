# Receipt: No Lateral Movement via Stolen Tokens

## Claim

HYP-03 is disproved: there is no evidence of lateral movement via stolen tokens to james.wu@acme.corp or maria.garcia@acme.corp. The malicious app's OAuth token usage was confined to sarah.chen@acme.corp's mailbox. No sign-ins from james.wu or maria.garcia using the malicious app ID were observed in M365 or Okta logs within the 72h window.

## Evidence

From QRY-20260328-001 (M365 identity, templates=4):

- Token refresh events for app 3fa85f64-5717-4562-b3fc-2c963f66afa6 (template 5a0c8e3f7b2d1694: "Token refresh for app <UUID> from <IP>") — all 3 refreshes originated from IP 185.220.101.42 and were scoped to sarah.chen@acme.corp's mailbox
- No consent grants for app 3fa85f64-... by james.wu@acme.corp or maria.garcia@acme.corp
- No sign-in events for james.wu or maria.garcia from IP 185.220.101.42 or any other Tor exit node
- james.wu and maria.garcia show only normal sign-in patterns (template 8f2d6a4e1b7c3950) and failed sign-ins (template 3e9b5d7a2c8f1046)

From QRY-20260328-003 (Okta identity, templates=3):

- james.wu: 218 sessions, all from New York IPs, all Okta Verify MFA, no anomalous OAuth grants
- maria.garcia: 190 sessions, all from Chicago IPs, all Okta Verify MFA, no anomalous OAuth grants
- No Okta system log events for either user involving the malicious app or Tor exit node IPs

## Confidence

Medium — Within the 72h observation window, there is no evidence of lateral movement. However, the attacker obtained Contacts.Read permission from sarah.chen's mailbox, which could be used for future targeting. A 72h window may be insufficient to observe slower lateral movement campaigns. Recommendation: extend monitoring of james.wu and maria.garcia for 30 days post-remediation.

## Notes

- The pack progression `phish-to-consent-to-takeover` does not have a defined step for lateral movement — it ends at mailbox_tampering (step 3). The absence of lateral movement is consistent with this being an email exfiltration attack rather than a broader account takeover campaign.
- The phishing email was delivered to all three users, but only sarah.chen clicked the consent link. This suggests the attacker used a spray-and-pray approach rather than targeted spear-phishing.
- The Contacts.Read permission remains a residual risk. Even without lateral OAuth movement, the attacker could have harvested sarah.chen's contact list for future campaigns outside the current observation window.
